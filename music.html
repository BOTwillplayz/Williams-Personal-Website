ARCANE:

Arcane League of Legends (Soundtrack from the Animated Series):

Playground (Bea Miller)

Very strong start to this album and the show arcane in general, I like the dark tone of the song and (I think) it is percussion instruments that really make this song stand out to me, it is an amazing song truly leaving little to be desired, although that doesn’t mean to me it is a great song, although it has its merits I would hardly say it is repayable or amazing song writing, but the vocal performance Miller gives is commendable, leading to award it 67/100.

Our Love (Curtis Harding & Jazmine Sullivan)

Woah, what a song, one if the most underrated songs on the album with its throwback style and flipping incredible vocal performances, the chill vibe and aura of the song both make me feel like I am happy on a rainy day and crying at my grandma’s house at once. The replayability on this track is VERY sadly lacking leaving it with an amazing 79/100 (I tip my hat to Harding and Sullivan, their performance was *chefs kiss*

Goodbye (Ramsey)

This song unfortunately falls flat in terms of its predicable sound and nature leading it to be one of the more forgettable songs of the album with it being rather dull and uninspired, although there is a silver lining and that is Ramsey’s excellent vocal performance, all in all I gave this song a deserving 51/100

Dirty Little Animals (BONES UK)

What a vibe of a song, really really awesome. Thats about it lads 73/100 (if you are wondering about the very high score it comes from the replay value in addition to the originality, something I think especially arcane artists forget, which is why it is the true make or break of your score and where Ramsey and Bea Miller missed out)

Enemy (Imagine Dragons)

(Preface: Although I am the Number 1 Imagine Dragons Hater I tried not to let this bias get in my way) NONONONONO I HATE IMAGINE DRAGONS *dying*. Ok but credit where credit is due, I don’t hate this song with it being an absolute ear worm and having good production and vocal qualities, butttt with that being said that does not excuse that song of being my number 1 sin, coworker music, it’s the anthem of people who pretend they’re fighting anime battles, but those battles are mostly passive-aggressive Teams messages. In conclusion it is a 54/100 (by what grace of god is this higher than Goodbye by Ramsey I feel bad for my generation)

Guns for hire (Woodkid)

This is by far my favorite track on the album with next to no flaws, (and an awesome scene in arcane but that is irrelevant), the replayability and emotional connection on this song are incredible leading me to award it a prestigious award as the first song on my 85/100+ playlist getting an astonishing 86/100

Misfit Toys (Pusha T)

Oh this track just reeks of arcane and I live for it, it is definitely on of the best songs off of this album with Pusha T giving a good performance but the biggest credit going to Sebastien Najand and Alex Seaver, who are the composers, lyricists and producers who did the heavy lifting on this song. 68/100

Dynasties and Dystopia (Denzel Curry, Gizzle & Bren Joy)

What an awesome song, the only issue is I liked more before the singing started, which is not something you want, although this song has really good replay value. At the 1:02 mark is my favorite part of the song with the awesome beat and best vocals coming to fruition. 65/100

Snakes (PVRIS, MIYAVI)

With the opening sounding vaguely like a song from wreck it ralph as opposed to arcane this song simply falls to the despair pit that is forgettability, on another sound track it may of stood out but on this soundtrack it falls so flat, now that doesn’t mean that this song doesn’t have its moments, I just that this song is unbelievably MID. 50/100

When Everything Went Wrong (Fantastic Negrito)

THIS! THIS is how you do country music, awesome, flipping amazing it has this perfect chaotic energy and perfect vocals, it is so Elvis coated and I love it. 71/100

What Could Have Been (Sting & Ray Chen)

Woah this song is by Sting!? My parents love that dude, this song must be amazing, *puts in headphones* Wow who could have guessed! It is mid as hell and sounds just like every other song on the arcane soundtrack, its only redeeming characteristic is the fact that the violin in this track is just to incredible. 9 year old William would have liked it alot more: 55/100
